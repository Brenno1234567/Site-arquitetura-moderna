ARCHITECTURE AND TECHNOLOGY:

Innovation and partnership that transform spaces and lives

Architecture goes far beyond the construction of buildings. It creates environments that welcome, function, and inspire. With technological advances, projects have become more precise, faster, and more sustainable, allowing ideas to be transformed into reality more efficiently.

WHAT IS ARCHITECTURE?

Architecture is the art and technique of designing spaces that meet the needs of people and society. It involves creativity, aesthetics, functionality, comfort, and safety.

More than building, architecture transforms ideas into environments that improve quality of life while always considering social and environmental impact.

IMPACTS AND CHANGES

Technology began gaining space in architecture during the 20th century with technical drawing software (CAD). Today, new digital tools have evolved into 3D modeling and intelligent systems such as BIM.

  • • Greater precision and speed in projects
  • • Realistic visualization before construction
  • • Reduction of waste and costs
  • • More efficient communication with clients
  • • Better use of resources and time

Architects can anticipate problems and ensure more sustainable results from the design phase onward.

TECHNOLOGY IN ARCHITECTURE

1. IDEA

Everything begins with an idea, inspired by the needs and dreams of the client.

2. STUDY

Analysis of the site, regulations, references, and project feasibility.

3. DESIGN

Development of plans, façades, and technical details with the support of technology.

4. SIMULATION

Testing of lighting, ventilation, comfort, and performance before execution.

5. EXECUTION

Construction monitoring to ensure quality, deadlines, and cost control.

6. SOLUTION

Delivery of a functional, aesthetic, and sustainable space that enhances both the project and the people who use it.

INTERVIEW: DIANA GRAVA GOMES – ARCHITECT

1. Why did you choose this profession and how long have you been working in it?
Because it combines creativity, technical knowledge, and the desire to transform spaces. I have been working in the field for about 10 years.

2. What do you do in your daily work?
Project development, meetings, technical site visits, and the search for sustainable solutions.

3. What are the main challenges?
Balancing expectations with budgets and deadlines, while keeping up with new technologies.

4. Is the market doing well?
Yes, it is thriving. Areas in high demand include sustainability, interior design, and BIM technology.

5. What skills are needed to stand out?
Creativity, technical expertise, communication, organization, and a sustainable mindset.

6. What advice would you give to beginners?
Love what you do, keep learning, and stay curious. Persistence and passion make all the difference!
